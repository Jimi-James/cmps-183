Lesson 1.1: Rhythm

Rhythm in music is everything related to speed and timing. For music notation, one of the most important aspects of rhythm is the measure, or bar. The measure is just a section of the staff. Its meaning is decided by the time signature, which is written at the beginning of the staff as two numbers: one on top of the other, often a 4 over another 4 (if there's just a big "C", that means 4/4). The number on top is how many beats there are per measure, and the one on bottom is the meter, or what type of note gets the beat.

If the number on top is a 4, that means there are 4 beats per measure. If it's a 6, then 6 beats per measure. It's self-explanatory.

The meter is slightly more complicated. If the number on the bottom is a 4, that means each quarter note is worth 1 beat. If it's a, 8, then it's each 8th note. 16 means each 16th note, and so on. In the other direction, 2 means half notes and 1 means whole notes.
